Disaster Crisis Identification from Twitter postsbased on Deep Learning Models

introduction


The word ”Disaster” implies a sudden overwhelming and unforeseen event that influences a large number of people around the world and require quick assistance for lessening the consequences. It requires rapid response and long-term support. Whenever a disaster breaks out, the top priority is to save the victims involved immediately, for which the news has to be disseminated widely. Forecasting disasters will serve as a very powerful tool since it reinforces the alleviation of the loss of damage to mankind and nature. Social media platforms have allowed people around the world to share information and communicate with their peers and specific audiences. A social media post on a disaster that is currently happening may bring awareness and thus help save a lot of lives. Given the vast amount of information on social media with the monotonous posts daily, finding relevant information in an effective manner has proved problematic since too much information can cause information overload and inability to separate relevant information from the irrelevant ones. We can use deep learning to effectively classify the posts on social media (here twitter)using text classification based on word embeddings. The most relevant posts for a disaster can be expeditiously separated from the non-relevant ones this way and made to reach the disaster emergency response squad and bring help to the victims at the earliest.

problem statement


Social media is highly used as a platform whenever an outbreak occurs. Not onlydoes it spreads the word quick, but also helps in escalating awareness thus lettingthe emergency services reach the location in no time for helping the victims. Butthe Data produced through micro-blogging platforms, like Twitter, has a hugevolume of information and data, that may or may not be relevant to an immediatedisaster. Hence, using social media as a tool, we identify the Relevant and Non-Relevant posts on Disasters to organize relief efforts, find help, and potentiallysave lives.

Objective

  1. To develop an efficient Disaster crisis identification model by using socialmedia posts from twitter.
  2. To Construct dataset from social media with bi-class categorization.
  3. To perform data pre-processing using Word embeddings with GloVe.
  4. To adopt different deep learning models for training.
  5. To assure the robustness of the validation and to prevent overfitting.

CONCLUSION


Through this project, we tried to conduct a survey on different techniques for classifying social media posts using deep learning on these posts. Social media has played a major role in disaster impact prevention by awareness raising, knowledge sharing, and bringing stories to the public as soon as possible, which opens an opportunity to approach and provide aid to the victims. A major disaster such as earthquake, flood or fire etc, requires immediate assistance from national or international levels to rescue as much lives as possible. Being able to fish out the posts that are relevant to a prevailing disaster from the huge volume of On-line posts on Social media provides a greater advantage. With the large volume and unstructured format of social media data, posts classification techniques development proves essential in the efficient timely intervention to avoid casualties. We adopt Deep Learning models for developing practical solutions to identify the relevant posts from social medias to in critical needs. Thus, the approach for bi-class classification and identification of a disaster occurence through social media posts with Deep Learning models has been pro-posed.
